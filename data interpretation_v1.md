⭐ Executive Overview - Page 1 from the dashboard

1) Card Charts only for the year 2025
- Net Income  
Societe Generale closes 2025 with a Net Income of €6.0 billion, marking a strong recovery phase after the volatility observed in 2020–2022. This level of profitability reflects improved operational efficiency and a more favorable macro‑financial environment.

- Total Revenues  
Total Revenues reach €25.78 billion, continuing the upward trajectory from 2021 onward. The bank consolidates its revenue base through both interest‑related and fee‑based activities.

- Return on Assets (ROA)  
ROA increases to 0.45%, the highest level in the 2016–2025 period. This indicates improved asset productivity and a more efficient balance‑sheet structure.

- Return on Equity (ROE)  
ROE rises to 8.84%, reflecting stronger internal capital generation and improved profitability relative to equity.

- Loan to Deposit Ratio (LDR)  
The ratio stands at 74.6%, indicating a conservative lending position relative to the deposit base. This supports liquidity resilience and aligns with post‑crisis prudential expectations.

- Tier 1 Capital Ratio  
Tier 1 Capital Ratio remains robust at 16.01%, well above the regulatory minimum of 6%. This highlights strong solvency and capital adequacy.

- Total Capital Ratio  
The Total Capital Ratio reaches 18.57%, maintaining a comfortable buffer above the 8% minimum requirement.


2) Line Charts for the period 2016-2025
- Net Income - Annual Trend  
The Net Income trend shows three distinct phases:
2016–2019: Stable profitability around €3-4 billion.
2020: A sharp decline into negative territory (‑€0.26 billion) due to pandemic‑related credit costs.
2021–2025: A strong recovery, culminating in €6 billion in 2025, the highest in the series.
This pattern reflects the bank’s resilience and effective post‑crisis restructuring.

- Total Revenues - Annual Trend  
Revenues remain relatively stable between €23–25 billion, with a temporary contraction in 2020.
From 2021 onward, revenues grow steadily, reaching €25.78 billion in 2025, supported by both interest and non‑interest income.

- ROA - Annual Trend  
ROA mirrors the profitability cycle:
Declines sharply in 2020 (0.01%).
Recovers gradually to 0.45% in 2025, indicating improved asset efficiency.

- ROE - Annual Trend  
ROE follows a similar pattern:
Drops to 0.29% in 2020.
Recovers strongly to 8.84% in 2025, reflecting strengthened earnings relative to equity.


3) Donut Chart - Revenue Composition only for the year 2025  
In 2025, the bank’s revenue structure remains well‑balanced:  
•	Net Interest Income: €10.07 billion  
•	Non‑Interest Income: €17.18 billion  

Interpretation:    
Non‑interest income accounts for roughly two‑thirds of total revenues, highlighting the bank’s diversified business model and reduced dependence on interest‑rate cycles. This composition is typical for large universal banks with strong fee‑based and market‑related activities.

⭐ Profitability & Income - Page 2 from the dashboard

1) Stacked Column Chart with the indicators Net Interest and Non‑Interest Income (period 2016–2025)

The stacked structure reveals:  
•	Net Interest Income remains relatively stable (approx. €9.8 - 11.2 billion).  
•	Non‑Interest Income fluctuates more significantly, driving most of the variation in total revenues.  
•	The 2020 dip is visible in both components, but the recovery afterward is driven mainly by Non‑Interest Income, which reaches its peak in 2025.  
This visual demonstrates the bank’s reliance on diversified income sources and the stabilizing role of interest income.


2) Line Chart with the indicators Total Revenues and Non‑Interest Expense (period 2016-2025)  
The relationship between revenues and expenses shows:  
•	Expenses remain high but stable, between €16–18.5 billion.  
•	Revenues consistently exceed expenses, ensuring positive operating margins.  
•	The gap widens after 2021, contributing to the strong profitability rebound.  
This trend indicates improved cost discipline and operational efficiency.  


3) Card Chart with the indicator Net Interest Margin Share % for the year 2025  
The Net Interest Margin Share in 2025 is 39.06%, reflecting the proportion of revenues generated from interest‑related activities. This level is consistent with a universal banking model where fee‑based activities play a dominant role. The stable margin share also suggests balanced exposure to interest‑rate movements.
